I'm interested in the social aspects of technology and how this is applied to marketing.

Here’s what I said about the web’s impact on this at a recent speaking engagement:

"In simplistic terms we’ve always known people talk about the decisions they make in their lives... the shift has been that technology has turned what was once ephemeral into something that is publicly searchable and retrievable, amplifying the potential effect of an opinion to include groups of people potentially only tangentially related to one another... as well as allowing these opinions to persist over time, e.g. through related web searches."

TWENTY THOUSAND LEAGUES specialises in innovative decision assistance marketing for higher education student recruitment.

I set this business up when it became apparent that the removal of the cap on student control numbers would increase competition betweeen universities and as such they would face challenges in marketing to prospective students. What concerned me was that the higher education sector would find itself exposed to the smoke-and-mirrors that plagues the rest of the marketing sector in relation to online advertising. TWENTY THOUSAND LEAGUES is a response to that.
